The biggest users of private health insurance in Australia are those aged 55 and older. Yet their fear of being underprotected and insecure means they’re likely to be stuck with outdated policies that they’re simply paying too much for.

So how can Aussies make sure they’re insured for the worst without overpaying for extra cover they don’t actually need?

New comparison service HealthInsuranceComparison.com.au is giving baby boomers a chance to make real savings on their premiums – in fact, our customers saved an average of $375.44 on their yearly premiums last March.

Health insurance premiums rose by a staggering 4.8% in April this year, a rise that continued a streak of constant increases since 2010. But in spite of this punishment older Australians aren’t updating their policies because they struggle to make sense of an increasingly confusing industry.
